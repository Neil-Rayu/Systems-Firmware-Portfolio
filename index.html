About Me

Building the infrastructure of tomorrow

I'm a junior studying Computer Engineering at UIUC with a passion for low-level systems, firmware development, and cybersecurity. My work spans from FPGA design to kernel development, with a focus on building secure, performant infrastructure at scale.

Currently interning at Sandia National Labs on embedded security systems, and working with the FAST Lab on CXL memory architectures. I'm drawn to challenges that sit at the hardware-software boundary—where optimization matters most and security is paramount.

Looking ahead, I'm excited about the convergence of specialized compute (GPUs, accelerators) and rack-scale infrastructure. Whether it's through graduate research or industry positions at companies pushing the boundaries of systems architecture, I want to help shape the next generation of computing platforms.
